Security Insights

  • Use Passphrases

    Combine 4 random words (e.g., "Correct-Horse-Battery-Staple"). They are hard to crack but easy to remember.

  • Enable 2FA

    Always turn on Two-Factor Authentication. Even if a hacker gets your password, they can't get in without your phone.

  • Use a Password Manager

    Don't rely on your memory. Use tools like Bitwarden or 1Password to generate unique keys for every site.

  • Avoid PII

    Never use Personally Identifiable Information like dog names, birthdays, or street names.

  • Beware of Phishing

    Hackers often pretend to be IT support to ask for your password. Real companies never ask for it.
